The United States, Canada & the Caribbean Ticket Change Fees
There are no change fees for travel originating within the 50 United States, Canada, Puerto Rico and the U.S. Virgin Islands (excluding Delta Basic experiences). Please remember that you will still need to pay the difference in price if your new flight costs more than your original flight.
Outside the United States & Canada Ticket Change Fees

Third-Party Ticket Handling Charges

Additional third-party charges may apply to the following:

  • A traditional travel agency
  • An online travel agency
  • Another airline, including SkyTeam and codeshare partners
External Ticket Handling Charges That Will Not Apply

For the following, external ticket handling charges will not apply:

  • Tickets originally issued by Delta â booked through reservation sales, a ticket office, on delta.com or on the Fly Delta app
  • Involuntary changes like cancellations, delays or schedule changes
  • Tickets issued with military or government fares
  • Customers completing a SkyMiles upgrade
  • Same-Day Confirmed Ticket Changes
External Ticket Handling Charge

The $50 charge is:

  • Waived if the change is completed on delta.com in My Trips
  • Applied for tickets changed through Reservations Sales
  • Applied for tickets changed at one of our Ticket Office Locations

Terms & Ticket Validity

During the ticket change process, we will apply your ticketâs remaining value to your new trip.

For travel between and/or within Canada, United States, Puerto Rico and U.S. Virgin Islands, your ticket must be reissued and all travel completed within one year from the original date of purchase.

For all other travel, your ticket is valid for one year. If travel was started, your ticket must be reissued and all travel must be completed within one year of the start date. If travel was not started, your ticket must be reissued and travel must begin within one year of the original date of purchase.
